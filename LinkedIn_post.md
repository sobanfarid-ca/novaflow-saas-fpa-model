# LinkedIn post draft

As a newly qualified Chartered Accountant, I've spent the last few years close to financial reporting: recording what already happened. I've been working to move into FP&A, where the job is the opposite, understanding the operational drivers behind the numbers before they happen.

To deepen my understanding of driver-based financial planning, I built a portfolio project from scratch: a three-year SaaS financial planning model, with dynamic forecasting, scenario analysis, and an executive KPI dashboard.

A few things I set out to get right:

- Driver-based forecasting, not a static budget. One dropdown flips the whole model across Base, Optimistic, and Downside, and every sheet recalculates from a single scenario switch.
- SaaS-correct mechanics: customer and MRR roll-forwards per segment, and a real split between billings, recognised revenue, and deferred revenue. Enterprise bills annually upfront, so cash and P&L genuinely diverge.
- An executive dashboard built like an internal board report, not a spreadsheet dump: a three-band KPI hierarchy, a colour-coded scenario chip, and five clean charts, with the metrics investors actually ask about (NRR, LTV/CAC, CAC payback, Rule of 40) up top.

Building it shifted how I think. Less about recording results, more about the assumptions and drivers that produce them, and what breaks when churn moves a few points or hiring gets cut.
